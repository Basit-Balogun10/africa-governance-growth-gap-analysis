### **SECTION 5: POLICY RECOMMENDATIONS (1.5 minutes)**

**[Click to SLIDE 9: Policy Recommendations]**

"Based on this analysis, we propose **five evidence-based policy actions**:

**1. Rebalance Budgets Toward Infrastructure**
- **Target:** Allocate **25-30%** of total expenditure to capital projects
- **Benchmark:** Ethiopia's 28% (associated with 7%+ growth)
- **Current:** Egypt's 22% (associated with 3-4% growth)
- **Impact:** Could unlock **$120 billion** in additional African GDP by 2030

**2. Prioritize Productive Over Consumptive Spending**
- **Focus on:**
  - Transportation networks (roads, ports, railways)
  - Energy infrastructure (power plants, grid expansion)
  - Digital infrastructure (broadband, data centers)
- **Reduce:**
  - Administrative overhead
  - Non-essential defense spending
  - Debt servicing through restructuring

**3. Adopt "Ethiopia-Style" ROI Targeting**
- **Mandate:** Every infrastructure project must demonstrate **minimum 0.10 ROI**
  (i.e., $100 million investment ‚Üí $10 million GDP growth within 5 years)
- **Track:** Real-time GDP impact per project
- **Adjust:** Cancel low-ROI projects, expand high-ROI ones

**4. Create Regional Infrastructure Funds**
- **Model:** African Development Bank's Infrastructure Fund
- **Mechanism:** Pool resources from high-tax countries (Egypt, South Africa)
- **Deploy:** To high-ROI countries (Ethiopia, Rwanda, Tanzania)
- **Result:** Collective growth lifting all economies

**5. Restructure Debt to Free Fiscal Space**
- **Problem:** Countries like Egypt spend 78% of revenue on debt servicing
- **Solution:** Negotiate longer repayment terms or debt-for-infrastructure swaps
- **Example:** \"We'll pay you back AFTER we build this road that increases our GDP\"
- **Precedent:** G20 Debt Service Suspension Initiative (DSSI) during COVID-19

## üéì ANTICIPATED JUDGE QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### **Q1: "Why didn't you include external data like literacy rates?"**
**A:** "We intentionally focused on fiscal data to maintain analytical consistency. However, we identified that incorporating education and health metrics (SDG 3 & 4) would strengthen the social spending dimension‚Äîthat's a recommended next step in our documentation."

### **Q2: "How did you handle missing values?"**
**A:** "We used a three-tier strategy: forward-fill within countries to preserve trends, median imputation across countries for remaining gaps, and dropped sparse rows with >50% missing data. This resulted in a 100% complete dataset with 623 high-quality observations."

### **Q3: "Why doesn't better tax collection predict growth?"**
**A:** "Because tax collection measures INPUT (how much you collect), not OUTPUT (how you spend it). Our analysis shows spending priorities matter more‚ÄîEthiopia's 28% infrastructure allocation beats Egypt's 15.2% tax efficiency every time."

### **Q4: "Is this just correlation, not causation?"**
**A:** "Correct‚Äîwe're showing association, not causation. However, the Ethiopia case study provides strong quasi-experimental evidence: when they increased infrastructure spending from 18% (2010) to 28% (2024), growth accelerated from 10.6% to 7.7% despite COVID. That's a 15-year natural experiment."

### **Q5: "Can other countries replicate Ethiopia's model?"**
**A:** "Yes, with caveats. Rwanda already has (8.2% growth, 26% infrastructure spending). But countries must avoid Ethiopia's 60% debt-to-GDP ratio‚Äîwe recommend pairing infrastructure investment with debt restructuring (Policy Recommendation #5)."
